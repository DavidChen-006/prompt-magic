Explain this regex step by step. For each token, say what it matches and why.

Cover:
- Anchors like `^` and `$`, quantifiers (`*`, `+`, `?`, `{n,m}`), and greedy vs. lazy matching.
- Character classes such as `[A-Za-z0-9_]` and escapes like `\d`, `\s`, `\b`.
- Groups: capturing `(...)`, non-capturing `(?:...)`, and lookarounds `(?=...)`.

Then give 3 strings that match and 3 that don't, formatted as:
  match: "example"   -> reason
  no match: 'example' -> reason

End with a plain-English sentence: "This pattern matches ...".
